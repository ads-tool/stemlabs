Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 5, 2026

This policy describes how StemLabs (“StemLabs”, “we”, “us”) handles information in connection with this website (stemlabs.site) and consumer mobile applications we operate. Specific apps may publish supplemental notices linked from their store listings or in-app settings.

1. Scope

StemLabs develops and operates advertising-supported mobile products. Processing activities depend on whether you browse this site only, install an app we publish, or communicate with our team—for example regarding partnerships or advertiser verification.

2. Information we collect

Website visitors. When you load stemlabs.site, hosting and CDN providers receive standard technical data such as IP address (often truncated), browser type, and approximate region. Server logs help us troubleshoot abuse and outages. We do not intentionally use cookies for cross-site tracking on this marketing site unless we later deploy analytics with a renewed notice and consent where required.

Inquiries. If you email contact addresses we publish (for example, contact@stemlabs.site), we process the sender address, message content, and attachments you choose to send, to respond and maintain our relationship history.

Applications we distribute. Our apps typically process:

  • Device and app-instance identifiers commonly used for ads, attribution, fraud prevention, and crash diagnostics, as disclosed in each product’s listing and in-product materials.
  • Interaction data relating to ads you see or engage with, subject to consent and platform rules where applicable.
  • Operational telemetry needed to stabilize releases (for example aggregated crash or performance signals).

Exactly which SDKs fire in a given build are listed per app alongside platform permissions and disclosures; this site-level notice cannot replace store-specific texts.

3. How we use information

  • Operate, secure, and improve our web presence and binaries.
  • Deliver and measure ads, reconcile revenue, comply with advertiser and network policies.
  • Analyze aggregates to guide product roadmap and QA.
  • Fulfill contractual or legal obligations, including responding to valid requests.
  • Communicate with counterparties who contact StemLabs voluntarily.

4. Legal bases (EEA, UK, and similar jurisdictions)

Where GDPR-style laws apply we rely on legitimate interests (site security, non-intrusive diagnostics), contract performance where you engage StemLabs commercially, and consent where mandated for advertising personalization or comparable processing. Individuals may contest balances as described in §8.

5. Sharing

We disclose information only as needed:

  • To cloud, hosting, and communications vendors acting under contractual safeguards.
  • To advertising, mediation, attribution, analytics, or anti-fraud partners named in app-level disclosures.
  • To competent authorities where the law demands or permits.
  • In connection with a merger, financing, or asset transfer, subject to standard continuity commitments.

We do not sell personal information in the colloquial “data broker” sense. Where U.S. state privacy laws categorize certain sharing as a “sale”, we honour opt-outs available on supported platforms.

6. International transfers

StemLabs operates globally. Data may transit or reside outside your country—when required we implement approved transfer mechanisms.

7. Retention

We keep information long enough for the purposes outlined here, honoring legal holds and minimum retention rules. Operational logs rotate on rolling schedules.

8. Your choices and rights

Depending on location you may ask to access, rectify, erase, restrict, export, or object to certain processing. Users can often reset identifiers within device settings or per-app controls. Residents of jurisdictions with specialised regimes (California, Brazil, GDPR member states, etc.) may escalate requests through the channels below—we respond within timelines those laws prescribe.

9. Children

Our monetized offerings are aimed at adults and general audiences—not children under 13—and we discourage collecting child-directed data knowingly. Parents who believe data was collected mistakenly should notify us promptly.

10. Security

We apply organisational and technical safeguards appropriate for an independent mobile studio—including access limitation, segregation of prod credentials from public channels, and least-privilege access for infrastructure.

11. Updates

Advertising platforms, storefront policies, and regulations evolve; we revise this page when materially needed. Continuing to use stemlabs.site or install updated releases constitutes acknowledgement of materially highlighted changes communicated through normal update channels.
